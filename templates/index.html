Chest PainPain in the chest that worsens with coughing or deep breaths, caused by inflammation in the lungs​
Fatigue and WeaknessThe immune response to TB infection drains energy, causing lasting tiredness and weakness.
Things You Should Do
Seek Medical AttentionConsult a healthcare provider if you have symptoms like a persistent cough or unexplained weight loss. Early diagnosis is essential for effective treatment​
Get Tested:TB testing is crucial for early detection, especially if you're at high risk or have been in close contact with someone who has TB.
Wear a MaskWearing a mask helps reduce the spread of TB bacteria through respiratory droplets, especially in close-contact or crowded areas.
Cover Nose When SneezingUse tissues or your elbow to cover your mouth and nose, limiting the spread of respiratory droplets.
Things You Should avoid
Ignoring SymptomsDon’t overlook persistent symptoms like a prolonged cough, fever, or night sweats; seek medical attention promptly for diagnosis.
Close Contact with Infected IndividualsTB spreads through the air, so limit interaction with anyone diagnosed with active TB to prevent transmission.
Crowded and Poorly Ventilated SpacesAvoid being in crowded or poorly ventilated areas where TB bacteria can linger in the air.
Smoking and Excessive Alcohol UseBoth can weaken your immune system, increasing susceptibility to infections like TB.
