What are the main symptoms of TB?
The main symptoms of TB include a persistent cough, chest pain, night sweats, fever, and unintentional weight loss.
How is TB diagnosed?
TB is diagnosed through a combination of a skin test (Mantoux test), chest X-ray, and laboratory tests on sputum samples to detect the bacteria.
Can TB be cured?
Yes, TB can be cured with a proper course of antibiotics, usually over 6 to 9 months. It is essential to complete the full treatment to prevent drug resistance.
What is tuberculosis (TB)?
TB is a contagious disease caused by the Mycobacterium tuberculosis bacteria, primarily affecting the lungs but can also impact other parts of the body.Tuberculosis (TB) spreads through the air when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or talks. The bacteria are inhaled by those nearby, leading to potential infection.
